About Projects

Iter is a current student project undertaken by a team of 8.

Initial level designs for Iter were developed early using tools such as height maps (which would be used in the project) and pro-builder built assets similar in shape to the planned art assets to get a better understanding of gameplay flow, scale, cinematography and design for segments of the level so that they could be better refined without having to worry about art assets. Below shows how these early concepts were then developed into the actual game. Level Layout The original design (left) featured a straight isolated path for the player to travel down, this felt too linear and simple and was instead replaced for a more dynamic area with bends and height variation so the player could learn to slide and jump in the introduction area whilst maintaining the idea of following a path. The original layout (left) saw the player introduced very early to intense platforming mechanics, using water as purely a hazard. Further the level was severely drawn out and difficult to navigate and easy to get lost in. Compared to the 2nd design (right) this level is now whilst more linear, allows for exploration with a clearer path where the river acts as a point of reference to the player. Further, it reduces the amount of platforming, opting more for segments of sliding, which having been tweaked promotes more fun and momentum to the game. The second design itself has gone through several iterations where the red (1st) and blue (2nd) followed a much longer path that when reviewed in the context of the whole game added very little to the playerâ€™s experience and fluffed out the playtime without much reward. Thus, by cutting through using the simple tree barriers already in the game, the level was shortened, and the art team has been able to better focus on the environment in a shorter area. Further, more effort could be put toward revising and developing the sliding sections to feel more momentous and cinematic for the player. This level of Iter saw the player move through a more desolate area, filled with larger areas dedicated to ruins and caves, with the intent to show more environmental storytelling through the use of ruin assets. The original design (red) like the previous level felt overly long and extended whilst the new revision has allowed for a more focussed route for the benefit of the player and for greater detail and effort to be put into areas that matter more for the player, whilst still allowing them to explore other areas of the level such as the ruin town (circular area). It's changes like this which have seen increases interest when testing and more enthusiasm to continue given the pacing feels more in line for the game.
